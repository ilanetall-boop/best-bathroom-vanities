Things to Know Before You Buy

  • Budget the build time. A 30-inch RTA vanity cabinet takes one to three hours with a screwdriver; the 42-and-48-inch kits in this guide need a second person and a half day.
  • Depth decides fit. Our picks run from 18 to 24 inches deep. Measure the walkway and door swing as well as the wall width.
  • Confirm the box contents. Many vanity listings ship the cabinet alone; check whether the countertop and sink come included before comparing prices.
  • Read the materials line. Product names in this category say solid wood while spec sheets list MDF or engineered wood. Ask the seller when the two disagree.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does an RTA vanity cabinet take to assemble?

Plan for one to three hours for a 27-to-36-inch cabinet and a half day for the 42-and-48-inch models, working with a screwdriver and the included hardware. Lay out and count the parts before you start, since a missing cam or dowel discovered at step 20 stalls the whole build. Large flat packs also need a second person for the carry and the final positioning.

Are RTA vanity cabinets cheaper than pre-assembled vanities?

Usually, and the gap comes from shipping. Flat packs cost less to freight and survive transit better than assembled furniture, so sellers price them lower. The RTA vanity cabinets in this guide run from $209.99 for the SUNTAGE 30-inch to $1,043.10 for the DELUXE LIVING 48-inch, and comparable assembled cabinets at each size typically list for hundreds more.

What size RTA vanity cabinet should I buy?

Measure your wall and your floor before looking at prices. Width should fill the alcove with an inch or two of clearance: 27 inches for a powder room, 30 or 36 for most full baths, and 42 or 48 for a primary bath. Depth matters as much; our picks range from the 18-inch-deep Quicklock, which suits narrow rooms, to the 24-inch-deep HCIOAN sink base, which needs open floor in front of it.
